SiC Membrane

Silicone Carbide (SiC) membranes are advanced ceramic membranes used for microfiltration in water and wastewater treatment. These membranes are made from sintered silicon carbide, offering a robust, chemically inert, and highly porous structure. SiC membranes are typically used as pre-treatment for reverse osmosis (RO), tertiary filtration, or as part of MBR (membrane bioreactor) systems.

Source: Cembrane

Advantages and Applications
Silicon Carbide (SiC) membranes, like this from Cembrane, are highly durable ceramic membranes used in challenging water and wastewater treatment applications. They offer excellent chemical resistance, thermal stability (up to 800℃ dry), and mechanical strength. Whith high permeability, broad pH tolerance (0-14), and low fouling tendency, they provide long service life and easy cleaning, Common uses include ground water to potable water, wastewater reuse, seawater RO pre-treatment, cooling tower recovery, MBR systems, and oil & gas produced water treatment.

Iron Oxidation Systems

This advanced iron oxidation system removes dissolved ferrous iron from groundwater through a pressurized aeration process. By injecting finely diffused air into the flowing water, it achieves complete oxidation without the need for chemical additives, making filtration more efficient. Its compact and cost-effective design integrates seamlessly with pressure filtration systems, offering a reliable and eco-friendly solution for iron removal.

Source: ATOMERATOR™

Sea Water Reverse Osmosis Desalination Process

Seawater Reverse Osmosis (SWRO) is crucial for addressing global water scarcity, but traditional systems achieve only 35-45% recovery, wasting significant water as brine. This technology revolutionizes SWRO by increasing recovery up to 60%, using multi-stage RO, interstage boosting, and energy recovery devices (ERDs) to enhance efficiency. This innovation reduces brine discharge, lowers energy costs, and minimizes environmental impact, making desalination more sustainable and cost-effective for the future.
